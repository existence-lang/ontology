# Conceptual Existence

## [Ontology](./ontology.md)

The realm of [Existence](./existence.md) that is [abstract](./abstract.md) and [conceptual](./concept.md) rather than physical. Ideas, mathematical truths, logical structures, [languages](./language.md), and all [abstractions](./abstraction.md) reside in Conceptual Existence.

Conceptual Existence is the counterpart to [Physical Existence](./physical-existence.md). Together they span all of [Existence](./existence.md).

## [Axiology](./axiology.md)

Conceptual Existence is where all [models](./model.md), [definitions](./definition.md), and [stories](./story.md) live. Every [domain](./domain.md) model, every [philosophy](./philosophy.md), every [algorithm](./algorithm.md) is an [entity](./entity.md) within Conceptual Existence.

[Natural Law](./natural-law.md) operates in Conceptual Existence just as it does in [Physical Existence](./physical-existence.md) — logical consistency, mathematical necessity, and [pattern](./pattern.md) are its governing forces.

[Conceptual Systems](./conceptual-system.md) are the organized structures within this realm.

## [Epistemology](./epistemology.md)

### [Cultural](./culture.md) Definition

<a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/conceptual" target="_blank">conceptual (wiktionary)</a>

> Of, or relating to concepts or mental conception; existing in the imagination.

<a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/existence" target="_blank">existence (wiktionary)</a>

> The state of being, existing, or occurring; beinghood.

<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abstract_object" target="_blank">Abstract Object (Wikipedia)</a>

> In metaphysics, the distinction between abstract and concrete refers to a divide between two types of entities. Many philosophers hold that this difference has fundamental metaphysical significance.

<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Platonic_realism" target="_blank">Platonic Realism (Wikipedia)</a>

> Platonic realism is the philosophical position that universals or abstract objects exist objectively and outside of human minds. It is named after the Greek philosopher Plato who applied realism to such universals, which he considered ideal forms.

### [Pattern](./pattern.md) Expression

Conceptual Existence contains every [abstraction](./abstraction.md) that any [entity](./entity.md) has ever created or could create.

#### Mathematics

The number 7, the Pythagorean theorem, and the axioms of set theory all exist in Conceptual Existence — independent of any physical medium that represents them.

#### Language

Every word, grammar rule, and [meaning](./definition.md) resides in Conceptual Existence. A [language](./language.md) is a [system](./system.md) within this realm that enables [communication](./communication.md) in [Physical Existence](./physical-existence.md).

#### Scoped Conceptual Existence

As with all [Existence](./existence.md), Conceptual Existence can be [scoped](./scope.md). Mathematical Existence is Conceptual Existence scoped to mathematics. A programming language's type system is Conceptual Existence scoped to that [domain](./domain.md).
