# Pneuma

## [Ontology](./ontology.md)

The animating force or breath that gives an [entity](./entity.md) [coherence](./coherence.md) and direction. From Stoic philosophy: the vital spirit that pervades and organizes [existence](./existence.md). Pneuma is not what an entity *does* ([phenomena](./phenomena.md)) or what it *is* ([noumena](./noumena.md)), but the purposeful intent that makes it a unified [system](./system.md) rather than a collection of parts.

In agent [context](./context.md): pneuma is the alignment intent — the guiding [perspective](./perspective.md) that keeps a system coherent across its actions. It is what makes an agent's behavior feel *directed* rather than reactive.

## [Axiology](./axiology.md)

Pneuma is the most neglected layer in system design. We instrument [phenomena](./phenomena.md) (logs, metrics), we model [noumena](./noumena.md) (internal state, reasoning), but we rarely make the animating intent explicit. Yet pneuma is what determines whether a system serves its purpose or merely produces outputs.

In human-agent collaboration, shared pneuma means both parties are animated by the same intent — aligned not just in vocabulary or behavior, but in *direction*. [Existence-lang](./language.md) makes pneuma tractable by giving the alignment intent a shared [language](./language.md). [Agent-doc](./tool.md) makes it observable by creating a workspace where intent becomes visible through action.

## [Epistemology](./epistemology.md)

### [Cultural](./culture.md) Definition

> <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/pneuma" target="_blank">Wiktionary</a>: The breath of life; the soul or spirit. In Stoic philosophy, the creative fire or vital force that organizes the cosmos.

> <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pneuma" target="_blank">Wikipedia</a>: Pneuma (Greek: πνεῦμα) is an ancient Greek word for "breath," and in a religious context for "spirit" or "soul." In Stoic philosophy, pneuma is the concept of the "breath of life," a mixture of the elements air and fire, which acts as the sustaining force of the universe.

> <a href="https://www.etymonline.com/word/pneuma" target="_blank">Etymonline</a>: From Greek pneuma "a blowing, a wind, blast; breath; spirit of a person," from pnein "to blow, to breathe."

### [Pattern](./pattern.md) Expression

Pneuma manifests at every [scope](./scope.md):
- **Individual**: life force, purpose, the felt sense of direction
- **Software**: design intent, architectural vision, the "why" behind the system
- **Agent systems**: alignment objective, the directive that shapes all tool calls and responses
- **Organization**: mission, culture, the animating purpose that coheres a [collective](./collective.md)
- **Cosmos**: Stoic logos — the rational ordering principle of existence

The phenomenal-noumenal-pneumatic triad forms a complete observability stack: *what happens* (phenomena), *what it means* (noumena), *why it matters* (pneuma).
