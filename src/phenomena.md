# Phenomena

## [Ontology](./ontology.md)

The observable manifestations of [existence](./existence.md). What an [entity](./entity.md) can perceive, measure, or detect through its [perspective](./perspective.md). Phenomena are the [signals](./signal.md) that reach [consciousness](./consciousness.md) — the layer of [reality](./reality.md) accessible to observation and description.

In [system](./system.md) terms, phenomena are the outputs, behaviors, and states that are visible at a given [resolution](./resolution.md). They are what we can instrument, log, and share as evidence.

## [Axiology](./axiology.md)

Phenomena ground understanding in the observable. Without phenomena, [knowledge](./epistemology.md) has no anchor — it becomes pure [abstraction](./abstraction.md) without referent. In human-agent collaboration, phenomena are the shared observable layer: both parties can point to the same outputs, logs, and behaviors as common ground.

Most platforms only instrument the phenomenal layer. This is necessary but insufficient — phenomena tell you *what happened*, not *why* or *what it means*.

## [Epistemology](./epistemology.md)

### [Cultural](./culture.md) Definition

> <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/phenomenon" target="_blank">Wiktionary</a>: An observable event or occurrence. Plural: phenomena.

> <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phenomenon" target="_blank">Wikipedia</a>: A phenomenon is an observable event. In philosophical usage, the term came to mean an object as it appears to, or is constructed by, the mind, as distinguished from a noumenon, the thing-in-itself.

> <a href="https://www.etymonline.com/word/phenomenon" target="_blank">Etymonline</a>: From Greek phainomenon "that which appears or is seen," from phainesthai "to appear."

### [Pattern](./pattern.md) Expression

Phenomena manifest at every [scope](./scope.md):
- **Individual**: sensory experience, emotional response, conscious awareness
- **Software**: observable outputs, API responses, log entries, metrics
- **Agent systems**: tool calls, generated text, state transitions, user-visible behavior
- **Organization**: measurable outcomes, reported metrics, documented decisions
- **Science**: experimental observations, reproducible measurements

The phenomenal layer is where [communication](./communication.md) begins — shared observation creates shared [context](./context.md).
